Report a Missing Person

If you believe that someone is missing then please contact your local police station. Dial 101 and inform them you would like to make a missing persons report, alternatively you can attend your local police station. It is a common belief that you have to wait 24 hours before reporting but this is not true, you can make a report to the police as soon as you think a person is missing.

If you are extremely concerned for the welfare of the person then dial 999 and ask for the police. People who are deaf, hard of hearing or speech impaired can text 999.

The Missing Persons Ghana does not take missing person reports.

The following factsheet has been written for the families and friends of missing persons. It provides further advice on what to do when someone goes missing.

1. About the missing person

Full name and address

Age and date of birth

Physical description:
  • Height
  • Build (e.g. thin, medium, large)
  • Ethnic appearance (e.g. White, Asian, Afro-Caribbean)
  • Eye colour
  • Hair colour and style
  • Any glasses or contact lenses worn

Any distinctive features such as tattoos, scars, birthmarks or any other distinguishing features?

Do you have a recent photograph?

If the missing person has a mobile phone with them, write down the number and the network (e.g. MTN, Vodafone, Airtel) if you know it

Does the missing person suffer from any medical condition, including mental health conditions? Do they need medication for this and do they have it with them?

What are the circumstances of this person going missing and where were they last seen?
